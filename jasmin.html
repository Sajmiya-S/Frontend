Jasmine is a delicate, white flower known for its sweet and enchanting fragrance. Often blooming at night, it symbolizes purity, love, and grace. The tiny star-shaped petals give the plant a gentle and elegant appearance. Jasmine flowers are widely used in perfumes, essential oils, teas, and cultural ceremonies because of their soothing aroma. Their calming scent and simple beauty make jasmine one of the most cherished flowers around the world.



Scientific Description

Jasmine (Jasminum sambac), belonging to the Oleaceae family, is an evergreen shrub or vine known for its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers. It has opposite leaves and blooms with tubular flowers that open mostly at night. Jasmine thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is widely used in perfumes and teas.



Did You Know?

Jasmine flowers are most fragrant during the night.
They are used to make essential oils, teas, and hair adornments in many cultures.
In some countries, jasmine is a national symbol (e.g., Indonesia, Philippines).
It attracts nighttime pollinators like moths due to its strong scent.
