Daisy – The Bloom of Innocence and Simplicity



The daisy is a sweet and charming flower known for its white petals and bright yellow center. It symbolizes innocence, purity, and new beginnings, making it a favorite in gardens and floral decorations. Daisies open their petals with sunlight and close gently at night, giving them a lively, cheerful presence. Their simple beauty and fresh appearance bring a sense of peace and positivity wherever they grow.



Scientific Description

The daisy (Bellis perennis) is a small perennial herb from the Asteraceae family. It features a basal rosette of leaves and a flower head composed of white ray florets and a yellow center of disc florets. Commonly found in meadows and lawns, daisies reproduce through seeds and creeping rhizomes.



Did You Know?

The name “daisy” comes from “day’s eye” because it opens at sunrise.
Daisies can grow in almost any climate and spread quickly.
They symbolize innocence and are often used in childhood games like “He loves me, he loves me not.”
The entire daisy flower is actually a cluster of florets.
